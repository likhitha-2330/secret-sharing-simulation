Threshold Secret Sharing

A secret is split into n shares. At least m shares are needed to reconstruct it. Click participants to select, then attempt reconstruction.
 
n = 5 · threshold m = 3 · click nodes to toggle

Coalition Attack Simulation

A coalition (red) tries to reconstruct the secret. Coalitions smaller than m always fail; at m they succeed. Click nodes to form the coalition.
 
n = 6 · m = 3 · click nodes to add/remove from coalition

Randomized Protocol Rounds

Each round, the mediator flips a biased coin (Pr = α). The secret is only revealed when the coin lands heads. Players cannot tell which round is real.
 
α = 0.3 · expected rounds ≈ 1/α ≈ 3.3

Deviation vs Honest Play

What happens when a player deviates (sends a fake share)? The honest coalition reconstructs correctly; the deviator gains nothing and poisons the result.
 
n = 5 · m = 3 · P1 (red) may deviate by sending a wrong share

ICP Lie Detection

The Information Checking Protocol attaches verification tags to each share. If a player lies, verifiers detect it with probability ≈ 1 − 1/|F|.
 
ICP detection probability ≈ 1 − 1/|F| ≈ 99.95% for |F| = 2089

(k, t)-Robustness

The protocol tolerates t faulty players and k colluding players when 3(t + k) < n. Click players to mark them as faulty (✕), then test recovery.
 
n = 10 · m = 4 · condition: 3(t + k) < n · click nodes to toggle faulty
